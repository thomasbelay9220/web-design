About us

Oromo music is an integral part of the cultural identity and heritage of the Oromo people, who are the
largest ethnic group in Ethiopia and also have a significant presence in neighboring countries such as Kenya
and Somalia. With its rich history and diverse styles, Oromo music reflectsthe experiences, traditions,
and struggles of the Oromo people throughout the centuries.

Traditional Oromo music is deeply rooted in oral tradition and has been passed down through
generations. It encompasses a wide range of musicalstyles, including work songs, ceremonial music,
storytelling songs, and praise songs celebrating heroes, leaders, and events in Oromo history. Instruments
such as the traditional Oromo flute (known as Ulullee) and the single-stringed fiddle ("masinqoo") are
commonly used in traditional Oromo music, along with hand drums, rattles, and other percussion instruments.
